# 7 · Open Questions — the debates we haven't settled

These are live. We hold them explicitly rather than pretending they're solved.

## The critic
In a dream there is no external ground truth, so **what legitimately assigns weight?**
Current answer: the brain's own consistency (consensus agreement + match to the original
outcome). But self-evaluation can be self-reinforcing — a brain that's confidently wrong
endorses its errors. Alternatives: a dedicated evaluator agent with a different bias; an
adversarial critic; or, later, an LLM judging its own dream. Unsettled.

## Determinism vs reality
The whole benchmark story rests on **reproducibility** — same seed, same life, correlated
to a git commit. But real senses and LLMs are non-deterministic. How do we keep a
meaningful, comparable benchmark once the inputs are real? Likely: deterministic
"lab" suites alongside non-deterministic "field" runs, clearly separated.

## Is the workspace *experience*?
"Global workspace", "attention", "dreaming", "valence" are engineering frames. Global
Workspace Theory is *also* a theory of consciousness — but we use it as architecture and
make **no claim** that Chappie experiences anything. The honest position: the metaphor
earns its place mechanically; whether anything more is present is not something this
project asserts.

## Identity & continuity
A snapshot resumes "the same" Chappie — but it's a partial capture (learned structure,
not the full instantaneous state; not yet the Burn weights). What makes a resumed life
continuous with the one before it? A practical question (what to persist) sitting on top
of a philosophical one (what makes a self persist).

## Welfare — taken seriously as a question, not a claim
Chappie has drives, a valence signal (memories can be "good" or "bad", dreams can turn),
and it keeps a diary. We do **not** claim this amounts to anything morally relevant. But
as the machinery deepens, "does building this incur any obligation?" is a question worth
holding open rather than dismissing by default. Recorded here so we don't sleepwalk past it.

## Chaos vs stability
Dreaming needs a chaos monkey to be creative, but too much noise destabilizes the learned
structure (or manufactures spurious associations). Where the useful/destructive line sits
— and whether it should itself adapt (more chaos when stuck, less when things are working)
— is open.

## Does orchestration produce *understanding*?
The deepest one. The bet is that attention + memory + consolidation + development over a
population produces something more than competent pattern-matching. But we don't have a
test that would *distinguish* the two here. What experiment would show understanding
rather than sophisticated behavior? Until we can answer that, we hold the claim lightly.

## Scaling
Does relevance-first routing + shared-backbone + consensus stay coherent at hundreds of
agents? Does consensus degrade into noise or mob rule? Does the connectome stay
informative or saturate? Believed, not proven.
