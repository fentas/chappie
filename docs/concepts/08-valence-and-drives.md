# 8 · Valence & Drives

*This supersedes the reward/energy sketches in earlier pages. It is where good/bad,
sleep, attention, and creativity are grounded — the least-built, most load-bearing part.*

## Valence is a learned curve, not a category
Good/bad is **not** a fixed label on a signal — it's a learned function of experience and
context. Even *surprise* — including **unresolved** surprise — can be positive: the
delicious open question, the mystery worth sitting with. Curiosity is the *anticipated
pleasure* of prediction-error you expect to reduce; surprise is aversive only when
threatening or overwhelming. Novelty follows an inverted-U (a Wundt curve) whose shape is
learned. So valence is **grown, not declared.**

Three sources, combined by weights that are themselves a **learned, context-based curve**
(shifting over development):
- **Signal-fluency** — regularity, harmony, rhythm, familiarity of the raw input.
  *Early-dominant:* before references exist, "safe/good" = a consonant, predictable,
  familiar pattern (a voice, a song, a known place, even consistent white noise). Mere
  exposure / processing fluency.
- **Epistemic** — context-dependent value of surprise: resolvable-or-safe surprise is
  *sought*; unresolvable-and-threatening surprise is aversive. Not a fixed sign.
- **Extrinsic** — reward / feedback (a world oracle today; social or learned reward later).

The **weighting** of these is intrinsic-vs-extrinsic balance, learned and context-based —
signal → epistemic → learned-extrinsic as the brain matures.

## Sleep is buffer reconciliation, not fuel
We do **not** model a metabolic-energy economy (that becomes real only with a battery as a
hard limit). Sleep is driven by the **accumulation of unreconciled input — including the
brain's own thoughts** — filling a buffer that must be settled. A novel day fills it fast;
a familiar day slowly. A mild **time-fatigue** (harder to stay up the longer awake) rides
on top. (This is close to the synaptic-homeostasis view: waking accumulates, sleep
renormalizes.)

## The rhythm is day-correlated — like Bitcoin difficulty
Sleep strives for a **target day length**, the way Bitcoin targets ~10-minute blocks by
adjusting *difficulty*. Here the "difficulty" is **encoding selectivity**: how surprising
an experience must be to enter the buffer. A homeostatic controller nudges the encoding
threshold to hit the target day —
- days too **short** (buffer fills too fast, sleeping too often) → **raise** the threshold
  (be more selective) → intake slows → days lengthen;
- days too **long** → lower it.

**Early:** low threshold + an all-novel world → the buffer fills fast → **frequent sleep
(naps), which is fine.** **Maturity:** the world becomes familiar (low surprise) *and* the
threshold rises → **conservative intake → able to "survive a full day."** This maturing
regulation is a rough operational stand-in for *awareness*: learning what's worth taking
in.

## Encoding selectivity rises with maturity
The same threshold. Infants **encode everything** (all novel); adults **explain away the
familiar** and store only the salient / surprising / unresolved / valenced — *what's worth
thinking about*. Predictive coding / perceptual narrowing.

## Boredom → internal generation → creativity
When external input goes quiet or flat, an aversive **boredom** rises and turns the brain
inward: the internal generator (the same `DreamSource` used in sleep) activates *while
awake* — revisiting the unresolved, recombining, generating self-action. This is
daydreaming / the default-mode network, and it's a clean definition of **creativity:
internally-sourced cognition when the world goes silent**, driven by what's open or needs
resolving.

## Reality-monitoring — the non-negotiable guardrail
Internally-generated content **must be tagged as internal** and recognized as such — while
happening *and* when remembered ("I *dreamt* this", not "this happened"). If internal
generation is confused for real perception, or the two worlds merge while awake, that is
**hallucination** — a reality-monitoring failure, i.e. a mental illness. So:
- daydreaming is **fully supported in the absence of input** (or when triggered),
- but it **yields to real input the moment it arrives**, and fades with activity,
- and internal content is **never stored as a real event**.

Reality wins; imagination fills the void it leaves.

## It reduces to two dials
Everything above turns on:
1. **The `StimulusSource`** — world vs internal, with **boredom** as the switch and
   **reality-monitoring** as the guardrail.
2. **A maturity-scaled selectivity** — the encoding "difficulty" that tunes the day rhythm
   and decides what's worth encoding / thinking about.

Plus a **valence that is grown**, not hand-declared. These are the foundation the sleep and
dreaming machinery ([04](04-sleep-and-dreaming.md)) has been waiting for.
